Exploratory Analysis



  • Terrorist Attacks around the world

    The pie chart above shows the distribution of terrorist attacks across the world. It is evident that the Middle East and North Africa has the highest number of terrorist attacks. This could also be due to the fact that there are more terrorist groups in that area.

  • Distribution of Weapon

    The most popular weapons are explosives, bomb, and dynamites followed by firearms. Thus, authorities can make use of this to better prepare themselves and train to fight such weapons.

  • Number of Attack Per Year

    From the graph above, it is also evident that the number of terrorist attacks have been increasing across the years and peaked in 2014. This shows the the importance of placing more resources to fight the terrorists.

  • Number of People Killed per Attack

    The figure above is an interactive visualisation that allows users to explore the world map based on the number of casualties from terrorist attacks. As shown in the screenshot below, Iraq suffered the most number of deaths from 1970 to 2015 with a total of 58.8k deaths.

  • Number of Failed Attack

    Another interesting thing we noted from the above visualisation was that the hotspot of high casualties tend to be within the same region. This is highlighted in a second screenshot in the figure below.

  • Number of Failed Attack

    The number of failed attacks have also been increasing since 1985. The total number of failed attacks peaked in 2015. Thus, this could possibly imply that the securities have been tightening and authorities are more prepared to handle and prevent terrorist attacks.

We plotted the violin plots for the important attributes against multiple; i.e. whether there were multiple attacks. From the first plot, we can see that generally if there was property damaged, as opposed to no property damaged, there is a chance that there will be a second attack. From the second plot, we can see that if property was damaged and the property damaged was likely more than USD$ 1 billion, all the cases have had subsequent attacks. Thus, if there are future huge catastrophic destruction of property, it is very likely that there will be another attack.
